---

## 1. Executive Summary

The UniKey Protocol Suite is a foundational decentralized trust layer designed for the "Machine Economy." It provides the cryptographic substrate required for AI agents, hardware devices, and payment networks to verify identity, authority, and intent at internet scale without centralized intermediaries.
This document serves as the authoritative index for all UniKey RFCs (Requests for Comment), providing a logical map of the architecture and the current status of each specification.

---

## 2. Architectural Layers

The documentation is organized into functional layers to support modular implementation and long-term extensibility:

- 1000 Series (Core & Architecture): The "Laws of the Land." Foundational semantics and logic.
- 2000 Series (Protocol & Data Formats): The "Mechanical Specs." Wire formats and encoding rules.
- 3000 Series (Verification & Trust Discovery): The "Network Layer." How trust is found and hardened via DNS/DKIM.
- 4000 Series (Conformance & Testing): The "Auditor's Tools." Pass/fail criteria for implementations.
- 5000 Series (Operational Guidance): The "Manuals." Integration profiles for OEMs and Enterprises.
- 9000 Series (Informational): The "Context." Rationale and competitive comparisons.

## 6. Security Considerations

The UniKey Protocol Suite adopts a Fail-Closed, Zero-Trust posture. Every RFC in this index is designed to operate in adversarial network conditions. Security is derived from cryptographic proof, not network location or privileged access.
